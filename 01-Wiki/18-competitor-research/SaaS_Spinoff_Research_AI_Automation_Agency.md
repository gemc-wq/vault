**What it does:**
- Aggregates sales data from multiple channels

## Business Model Options

### Option A: Point Solutions (Recommended)
**Approach:** Launch each automation as standalone SaaS product

**Pros:**
- Focused value proposition
- Easier to market
- Can test demand per product
- Lower customer education needed

**Cons:**
- Multiple codebases to maintain
- Separate marketing for each

**Pricing:** $99-$999/month per product

---

### Option B: All-in-One Platform
**Approach:** Single platform with all automations

**Pros:**
- Higher ARPU (average revenue per user)
- Stickier product
- One relationship to manage

**Cons:**
- Complex to build
- Harder to sell (too many features)
- Higher churn risk if one feature breaks

**Pricing:** $499-$2,499/month tiered plans

---

### Option C: Hybrid Agency + SaaS
**Approach:** Services + software bundle

**Pros:**
- High-touch onboarding
- Customization revenue
- Better customer success

**Cons:**
- Doesn't scale as well
- Services margin pressure

**Pricing:** Setup $5K-$25K + $299-$999/month SaaS

## Technical Architecture

**Stack:**
- Frontend: Next.js + Tailwind
- Backend: Node.js / Python (FastAPI)
- Database: PostgreSQL + Redis
- AI: Gemini API, OpenAI, custom models
- Hosting: Vercel + AWS
- Auth: Clerk or Auth0
- Payments: Stripe

**Key Considerations:**
- Multi-tenancy from day 1
- API-first design
- Webhook-heavy (Shopify, Amazon integrations)
- Scalable job queue (Bull/Redis)

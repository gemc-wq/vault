# GoHeadCase — Team Mission + Highest-Probability Strategy (v1)

## Context / Constraints
- Licensed print-on-demand tech accessories.
- Lower price point vs Casetify, but royalties apply.
- Amazon fees are high (~20–25%); gross margin after Amazon fees approx **33%**.
- We can achieve profit if we can sustain **~20–25% marketing budget** while maintaining disciplined CAC.
- We are **licensed-first** (customers often arrive with IP intent). We still need a clear conversion reason to choose us over official stores/Amazon/random sellers.

## Highest-probability strategy (short-term → long-term)

### Decision #1 — Positioning: what is most likely to work?
**Recommended north star (highest probability):**
> **Licensed-first + performance-first:** “The safest, easiest place to buy the *right* officially licensed case (for the exact device), delivered fast, at a better price.”

**Why this maximizes probability under our economics:**
- Converts existing intent (fans searching, gift buyers, marketplace traffic).
- Creates a moat that is not merely “we have the license”:
  - device coverage
  - protection proof
  - delivery & returns reliability
  - authenticity trust
- Improves conversion → lowers CAC → makes a 20–25% marketing budget feasible.

**Casetify-style brand-first** is a compounding bet, but lower probability in the short term unless the conversion machine + asset system is already world-class.

### Decision #2 — Short-term “fans” play: what’s the highest-probability execution?
Influencer marketing is the right lever, but only if the landing path is frictionless.

**Recommended short-term stack:**
1) Pick **one universe** to win first (avoid splitting focus).
2) Build one tight landing experience per universe:
   - Shop by IP → shop by device → best sellers → trust proof → buy
3) Run micro-influencers + affiliate codes:
   - many small creators > a few big ones (until creative is proven)
4) Add retargeting + email/SMS capture:
   - gift reminders, drops, back-in-stock

## Gaming accessories (notes)
- Opportunity: competitor (Skinit) is not a gold-standard ecommerce experience.
- We can outclass on:
  - merchandising clarity (shop by device + accessory type)
  - premium macro photography
  - device finder UX
  - trust proof (materials, fit, warranty)
- Recommendation: don’t mix accessories into the main template until phone cases are nailed; add later as a module or its own universe.
